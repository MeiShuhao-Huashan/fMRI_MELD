3.5 rs-fMRI model ablation and representative cases
To validate the architectural components of the functional model, we performed an ablation analysis on the revised scope38 cohort (n=38) (Supplementary Table S2). Rescue was computed relative to the T1-only baseline within this cohort, focusing on the subset of baseline T1 detection failures under the boxDSC endpoint (Det(boxDSC>0.22); n=17). Within these 17 cases, we quantified rescue on (i) Det(boxDSC>0.22), (ii) Det(PPV-in-mask≥0.5) among baseline PPV failures (n=15), and (iii) pinpointing among baseline pinpointing failures (n=14).

Across ablations, the canonical fMRIhemi configuration (B2a; embed+FC) achieved the strongest rescue on the boxDSC endpoint, recovering 58.8% (10/17) of T1 detection failures, and also achieved strong rescue on pinpointing (28.6% [4/14]) and PPV (13.3% [2/15]). Removing embeddings (B2b; FC-only) substantially reduced rescue across endpoints (29.4% [5/17] for boxDSC rescue; 6.7% [1/15] for PPV rescue; 7.1% [1/14] for pinpointing rescue), supporting the importance of pretrained embeddings and the dual-branch design.

One variant (A1) ranked higher by the mean rescue rank due to its improved PPV rescue (26.7% [4/15]); however, this came with a markedly higher false-positive burden (1.711 FP clusters/pt; precision 0.167), limiting deployability. We therefore retained B2a as the deployable rs-fMRI baseline, as it provided the best overall trade-off between rescuing challenging cases (10/17 boxDSC; 4/14 pinpointing; 2/15 PPV) and maintaining a lower false-positive rate (1.079 FP clusters/pt; precision 0.262).
